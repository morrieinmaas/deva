What defines Poweryoga?




Poweryoga derived from the Ashtanga yoga, which has the same basics as Hatha Yoga. However, Hatha Yoga is more meditative, and keeps the poses for several minutes. With Poweryoga, the poses are in rapid succesion of another and we combine the breath to create fluidity and flow in movement. A typical yoga class starts seated and we build up towards a 'Climax', followed by a 'Shavasana', also known as relaxing-part of the class.

Poweryoga creates a lot of energy in the body, making it more easy for mind and muscles to release tension stored, creating a healing effect. It makes the body more flexible, more fit, and relaxes the mind. You create couplesse, strength and it enhances concentration. There is something in yoga for everyone.



Average build- up of the yoga class:

  1. 1. Welcome and meditation
  2. 2. Sun salutation A and B
  3. 3. Grounding
  4. 4. A variation of standing and seated poses
  5. 5. Relaxation
